Divergence of a Vector Field

Vector Operators: Both the divergence and curl are vector operators whose properties are revealed by viewing a vector field as the flow of a fluid or gas.

Intuitive Understanding: The divergence of a vector field is relatively easy to understand. Imagine a fluid flowing with velocity field \( \mathbf{F} \), where the fluid appears to be expanding outward from the origin.

\[ \nabla \cdot \mathbf{F} = \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial g}{\partial y} + \frac{\partial h}{\partial z} \]

Interpretation: The divergence of this vector field is positive, indicating expansion. Conversely, if the fluid compresses toward the origin, the divergence is negative.

Gradient Definition: If \( f \) is a scalar function, its gradient is given by:

\[ \nabla f = \begin{pmatrix} \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} \\ \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} \\ \frac{\partial f}{\partial z} \end{pmatrix} \]

Laplacian Operator: The divergence of a gradient is the Laplacian:

\[ \text{div}(\nabla f) = \nabla^2 f = \Delta f \]
